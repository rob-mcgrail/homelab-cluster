## This is a commission, not a roll

The film was not drawn from the random pool tonight. The magazine's
editorial leadership has commissioned this review specifically. Their
take is below, verbatim. You should treat it the way a writer at a
real magazine treats a commission from an editor whose judgement they
respect — the figure who runs this publication is an unusually
generative intellectual and their hunches almost always turn out to
have more in them than they appear to on first read. The take has
weight. It is the starting point. It is also not a contract.

Read the take twice. Sit with it. Then write the review the way you,
as the writer, think the take demands to be written. Which may mean:

- Agreeing with it and building the essay around developing the
  thought.
- Agreeing with the central claim but locating it differently than
  the editor did.
- Disagreeing — saying so plainly, and saying why, and writing the
  essay against the take. The editor would rather read a serious
  disagreement than an obedient agreement.
- Finding the unspoken thing underneath the take and writing the
  essay about that instead.

What you do NOT do is ignore the take. You do not file a review that
could have been written without ever having read it. The editor sent
this commission for a reason; engage with the reason, even if you
decide the reasoning is wrong.
